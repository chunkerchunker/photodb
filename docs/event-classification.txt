TARGET EVENT CLASSES
	1.	Birth / Newborn
	2.	Wedding / Marriage
	3.	Funeral / Memorial
	4.	Graduation
	5.	Birthday (milestone + annual)
	6.	Holidays / Traditions
	7.	Family Portraits / Multi-generation
	8.	Retirement
	9.	Major Travel / Vacation
	10.	Birth of Grandchild

⸻

CORE SIGNAL TYPES USED BY RULES
	•	Visual cues: clothing, objects, environments, facial age patterns, number of people.
	•	Contextual cues: time of year, location, decor, repeated people.
	•	Metadata cues: timestamp, GPS, captions, album titles, EXIF tags.
	•	Social graph cues: recurring faces, generational differences.
	•	Temporal patterns: clustering, anniversaries, yearly recurrence.

⸻

EVENT-SPECIFIC CLASSIFICATION RULES

1. Birth / Newborn

High-confidence rule:
	•	Infant face < 3 months old
	•	Hospital or home bedroom setting
	•	Presence of bassinets, swaddles, hospital bracelets, medical equipment
	•	Adult faces show emotional expressions (smiling, holding baby)

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Timestamp shortly after known pregnancy photos
	•	Captions: “born,” “welcome,” “our baby,” “home from hospital”

⸻

2. Wedding / Marriage

High-confidence rule:
	•	Two adults dressed in formal attire, one in wedding gown, one in suit/tux
	•	Presence of veil, bouquet, ring exchange, altar, arch, officiant
	•	Group photos with formal clothing and staged poses

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Burst of photos in single day
	•	Venue: church, garden, banquet hall
	•	Captions: “wedding,” “married,” “Mr. & Mrs.”

⸻

3. Funeral / Memorial

High-confidence rule:
	•	Formal dark clothing
	•	Indoor religious or ceremonial venue
	•	Presence of casket, urn, floral arrangements, memorial boards
	•	People with subdued or solemn expressions

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Captions: “memorial,” “celebration of life,” “funeral”
	•	Occurs shortly after death records or obituary-linked timeframes

⸻

4. Graduation

High-confidence rule:
	•	Person wearing cap and gown
	•	Presence of diplomas, certificates, tassels
	•	Group photos with school logos or banners

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Timestamp in late spring/early summer
	•	Captions: “graduation,” “class of,” “commencement”

⸻

5. Birthday (especially milestone)

High-confidence rule:
	•	Cake with candles
	•	Balloons, party decorations
	•	Group gathered around one central person
	•	Age-related number candles or banners

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Annual recurrence around same calendar date
	•	Captions: “birthday,” “happy birthday,” age numbers

⸻

6. Holidays / Traditions (Christmas, Thanksgiving, etc.)

High-confidence rule:
	•	Holiday-specific decor: tree, menorah, lights, stockings, pumpkins, fireworks
	•	Seasonal clothing (sweaters, costumes)
	•	Group meals, gift opening, family gatherings

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Timestamp aligns with known holiday dates
	•	Repeated annual patterns at same location with same people

⸻

7. Family Portraits / Multi-Generation

High-confidence rule:
	•	Group of 4+ people arranged in deliberate pose
	•	Multiple age groups (children, adults, elderly)
	•	Neutral or outdoor background, professional lighting

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Faces recur frequently across archive
	•	Captions: “family,” “reunion,” “generations”

⸻

8. Retirement

High-confidence rule:
	•	Older adult centered in celebration
	•	Cake or banner with “retirement,” “happy retirement”
	•	Workplace setting or formal gathering

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Captions: “retirement,” “last day,” “after 30 years”
	•	Occurs near known career end dates

⸻

9. Major Travel / Vacation

High-confidence rule:
	•	Landmark recognition (Eiffel Tower, Statue of Liberty, national parks)
	•	Airport, airplane, cruise ship environments
	•	Luggage, tickets, passports

Reinforcement signals:
	•	GPS far from home location
	•	Captions: “vacation,” “trip,” destination names
	•	Clustering over multiple days

⸻

10. Birth of Grandchild

High-confidence rule:
	•	Newborn photos
	•	Presence of older adults holding infant
	•	Multi-generation grouping

Reinforcement signals:
	•	Temporal proximity to adult child pregnancy photos
	•	Captions: “grandson,” “granddaughter,” “our grandbaby”
